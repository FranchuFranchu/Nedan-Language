Conversion

Verb to adverb: -eme Verb to noun: n-ida Verb to adjective: -ido Noun to adverb: -ane Noun to adjective: -ano Noun to verb: -ana Adjective to noun: n- -ena Adjective to verb: -eno Adjective to adverb: -enede

Modifiers

Simple Modifier It is an adjective or an article that modifies the main noun. It goes after the main noun. Compound Modifier Structure: Prepostion + Noun construction Example: e [mi nama] of [my bed]

Prefixes are used to indicate mood, evidentiality, word order and aspects.

Mood:

Indicative: an- Subjuntive: aen- Interrogative: oan- Negative versions of all those: no-[prefix]-

Evidentiality:

Indeterminate: en- Perception: iv- Deduction: ev- Hearsay: ov- Assumption: al-

Aspects:

Single: e- Geruind: o- Participle: a- Habitual: ao- State: oi

Imperative:

Non-imperative: nes- Imperative: nas-

Word Order

: VSO: la- VOS: le- SVO: lao- OVS: leo- SOV: leu- OSV: lau- Versions of them where the subject is inflicted: [prefix]-ve If no prefix is said, the first one of each list is assumed. Prefixes are said in this order: Mood Evidentiality Aspects Word Order Imperative If two consonants get together, an "e" is added between them Examples: To order (imperative) the listener to habitually eat: aovenasoeda To say that you believe in God aloiveeema Deus
